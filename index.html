THE FORMAT

HOW THE COWBOY POLO CIRCUIT WORKS

The Cowboy Polo Circuit is a stacked season of sanctioned chukkers under the United States Polo Patrons Association. A season is divided into rounds. Each round generates player and horse statistics which roll up into the Circuit Leaderboards. Rider handicaps, horse performance scores, and chapter participation are all tracked against a single framework.

At the end of each season, the final standings are snapshotted, prizes are awarded, and the core data can be committed on-chain for permanent record inside the Patronium ecosystem. This page is a mockup of how that ecosystem will be presented to riders, patrons, and arenas.

Season Sign-Up & Roles

Riders enroll to receive a provisional Cowboy Polo Circuit handicap and to be included in sanctioned match scheduling.

Horses are entered into the Circuit pool — either as part of the String 7̶7̶7̶ Remuda or privately owned — and begin to accumulate a performance trace: chukkers played, points contributed, and awards earned.

Host Arenas apply to carry official Circuit dates, submit results, and participate in seasonal prize pools for facilities that reliably grow the sport.

SEASON AWARDS

PRIZES OF THE COWBOY POLO CIRCUIT

Prizes are not random trophies — they are the visible, ceremonial expression of the same statistics that drive the leaderboards. Each award is tied to a measurable outcome, so there is a clear line from the chukker sheets to the season finale.

  • ★ HIGH-POINT RIDER OF THE CIRCUIT
    Awarded to the rider with the highest cumulative impact index across all sanctioned chukkers, factoring goals, assists, ride-offs, and sportsmanship adjustments.
  • ★ STRING 7̶7̶7̶ HORSE OF THE SEASON
    The horse with the best combined record of reliability, match impact, and rider evaluation inside the String 7̶7̶7̶ Remuda.
  • ★ MOST IMPROVED HANDICAP
    The rider whose handicap, and underlying metrics, show the clearest growth from the start to the end of the Circuit season.
  • ★ BEST NEW HORSE / OTTB TRANSFORMATION
    Recognising horses that successfully transitioned into polo work and consistently supported riders through the season.
  • ★ HOST ARENA OF DISTINCTION
    For facilities that keep fields safe, matches flowing, and riders coming back — measured through participation, reliability, and umpire feedback.
How Prizes Relate to the Leaderboards

Each prize draws directly from the same seasonal dataset. Instead of inventing separate “award criteria,” the Cowboy Polo Circuit treats the awards as narrative summaries of what the stats already say.

At season’s end, a snapshot is taken: standings, horse scores, arena participation, and growth metrics. That snapshot can be hashed and stored on-chain, so every award can be traced back to the recorded performance that earned it.

RESULTS & RECORD

SANCTIONED CHUKKERS & SEASON RECORD

Match captains or appointed officials submit chukker sheets: teams, scorelines, rider combinations, and notable horse usage. The system ingests those sheets, updates the standings, and prepares the seasonal data to be mirrored into the Patronium ecosystem.

Result Submission (Mock)

Chukker Sheet Fields: Date, host arena, chapter, teams, riders, horses, scoreline, and notes. In the live system, these would be entered or uploaded via a secure portal and verified before they touch the leaderboards.

At the close of the season, the full dataset can be compressed into a canonical record: a hash and summary stored on-chain, with human-readable tables presented here for patrons, players, and the public.
